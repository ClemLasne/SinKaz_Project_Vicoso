# The SIN-KAZ Project

The SINKAZ project aims at exploring **how hybridization disrupts patterns of gene expression**. To do so, 2 Artemia species *A. sinica* and *A. kazakhstan* were crossed to obtain F1 individuals, which were the back-crossed to the 2 parental populations to obtain "F2BC" individuals ("BC" standing for "form a Back-Cross"). 



RNA (and some DNA) dataset from Parental, F1 and F2BC populations were obtained over several years.

# The data

All RNA and DNA data used in this project is **whole body** sequencing.

## Before 2023 (Christelle and Slava's data)

### 1. Initial F2BC RNAseq data

The first batch of **RNAseq** data was obtained by Christelle and Slava. A sinica male was crossed with the kaz female. An F1 female produced by this cross was then back-crossed to her sinica father, and F2BC males and females were produced. From this experiment, the sinica father and the kaz mother were sequenced, 2 replicates each. Were also sequenced 10 males and 10 F2BC females.

## After 2022 (Clem's data)

The chromosome painting analysis of the F2BC individuals from Christelle and Slava's dataset raised some questions due an intriguing pattern: the absence of recombination between the sin and kaz genomes. Clem, Ari and Julia then set-up more crosses between sinica and kaz in order to sequence more samples.

### 2. F1 RNAseq

Reciprocal crosses between A. sinica and A. kaz were done in the lab and F1 males and females from both directions of the cross were sequenced (RNA):

**from sinica mother X kaz father (i.e. F1 SIN-KAZ)**
- 7 males
- 6 females

**from kaz mother X sinica father (i.e. F1 KAZ-SIN)**
- 6 males
- 5 females
